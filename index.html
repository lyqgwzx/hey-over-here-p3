“Hey, Over Here!”

A voice-summoned mobile waste-management robot for shared offices — studied as a controllable simulation.

Li Ya (李亚) AP0011 — Human-Computer Interaction Instructor Sahba Zojaji SUSTech · Shenzhen Loop Area Institute (SLAI)
▶ Try the live simulation Watch the demo Report & slides

Abstract

Summoning an in-office service robot today means opening an app and tapping through menus — extra steps that fragment focused work. “Hey, Over Here!” asks whether ambient voice summoning can replace those explicit interfaces while preserving user trust and fairness. The robot responds to a spoken “over here,” uses sound-source localization to estimate the caller’s direction, and resolves it against a semantic map of named workstations. We reproduce the scenario as a browser simulation and run a within-subjects study across three conditions — app baseline, voice-only, and voice + visual feedback — measuring interaction friction, calibrated trust (especially when localization fails), multi-user fairness, and cognitive load.

Four HCI objectives

FRICTION

Effortless summoning

Replace app taps with a single utterance — measured by actions and time-to-arrival.

CALIBRATED TRUST

Know what it heard

Light, direction beam and ETA so users can tell when localization is right — or wrong.

MULTI-USER FAIRNESS

No one ignored

Acknowledgment, queueing and ETA when several people summon at once.

COGNITIVE LOAD

Don’t make me think

No menus, names or coordinates to recall — measured with NASA-TLX.
